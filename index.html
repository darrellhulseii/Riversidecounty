Questions

Good to know

Are you the internet provider? +
No. Complete Home Today is an independent authorized sales agent and reseller. We help you compare options and sign up for service, while the provider you choose handles the network, billing, installation, and support.
Which speeds can I get? +
We offer 100% fiber internet from 200 Mbps up to 7 Gig, plus a wireless 5G home internet option (up to 300 Mbps) where fiber isn't available yet. The exact providers and speeds available depend on your address — that's what the availability check confirms.
What fees should I expect? +
Pricing isn't shown on this site — your agent reviews exact pricing and every fee with you before you commit. Depending on the plan, that can include taxes and surcharges, a monthly router/modem rental, a one-time install fee, and a processing and handling fee due at signup. AutoPay enrollment may be required for the best available rate.
Is there a contract? +
It depends on the plan. Some plans include a 12-month term, and an early termination fee may apply if service is cancelled within that period. Other plans, including wireless 5G home internet, require no contract. Your agent will explain the terms for whichever plan you choose.
Do you offer home security? +
Yes. We offer professionally monitored home alarm systems through Direct Protection Security, with three packages — Bronze, Silver, and Gold. Equipment and 24/7 monitoring are quoted for your home; just pick a package on the availability form and your agent handles the rest.
Is the pricing on this site final? +
Offers and pricing change periodically and vary by address. Your local Complete Home Today agent confirms exact, current pricing and terms for your home at the point of sale.

Rep FAQ

Questions about joining

Is this a W-2 job or 1099? +
This is a 1099 independent contractor role. You are not an employee of Complete Home Today LLC. There is no base salary, no guaranteed hours, and no employee benefits. You are responsible for your own taxes, transportation, and expenses. The engagement is structured in compliance with California AB5 (Labor Code §2775 et seq.) and the Borello multi-factor test — you control your own schedule, routes, and sales approach.
What products will I sell? +
Fiber internet (200M through 7 Gig) and home alarm systems through Direct Protection Security. Selling both on the same visit maximizes your per-door earnings. Competitive plans and current new-customer offers make the pitch easier.
How and when do I get paid? +
Commission is earned per confirmed installation — not per pitch, not per signature. Payment timelines and the full rate structure are outlined in your IC agreement before you sign. Nothing is verbal.
What if an install gets cancelled after I close the deal? +
Chargeback rules are defined in your IC agreement. You will know exactly under what conditions a commission is clawed back before you ever close your first deal. No surprises after the fact.
Do I need prior sales experience? +
No. We provide in-depth pitch training for those who need support and travel options. Prior experience helps, but what matters most is a self-starter mindset and willingness to put in the reps.
What territories are available? +
Wherever the providers we represent offer service. Because one of those providers offers internet in all 50 states, there's opportunity throughout the United States. Fiber-optic internet is more limited — it isn't offered in every territory — but the majority of California is covered, and fiber reaches a large and growing number of states. Your exact territory is set during your phone screen, based on your location and what's serviceable nearby.
Is this compliant with California AB5? +
Yes. The IC agreement is drafted in compliance with California Labor Code §2775 et seq. (AB5) and the Borello multi-factor test. As an independent contractor, you are free to set your own schedule, choose your own routes, and control your own sales approach. You are not subject to behavioral control by the Company.
