Frequently Asked Questions
Everything people in Moreno Valley, Perris, and Riverside ask about switching internet providers.
Is fiber internet available in Moreno Valley? ▼
Yes — both Frontier Fiber and AT&T Fiber are available in parts of Moreno Valley, covering approximately 56–79% of addresses depending on the provider. Frontier covers more of the city. The only way to confirm is to check your exact address, since fiber availability is street-by-street. Enter your ZIP above and we'll show you what's available.
What's the cheapest internet in Perris, CA? ▼
Mediacom's Access Internet 60 plan starts at $24.99/mo and is the cheapest option in Perris with 60 Mbps speeds. Spectrum Internet Advantage is $30/mo for 100 Mbps. For households that qualify (low-income, SNAP, Medicaid, K-12 students), Spectrum Internet Assist can be free or near-free. Check the Perris tab above to compare all current plans.
Can I get internet with no credit check in Riverside County? ▼
Yes. EarthLink offers fiber and 5G internet plans with no credit check required in all three cities — starting at $39.95/mo for 100 Mbps. T-Mobile Home Internet and Starlink also don't run credit checks. These options are ideal if you've been declined by Spectrum or AT&T.
Does Starlink work in Moreno Valley, Perris, and Riverside? ▼
Yes — Starlink has 100% coverage in all three cities. It's the best satellite option, delivering 50–250 Mbps with ~25ms latency (much lower than older satellite providers like HughesNet or Viasat which average 600–800ms). You'll need a one-time hardware cost of $349 (or as low as $89 in low-congestion areas) plus $90/mo. There's a 30-day free trial.
What internet provider has the best customer service in the Inland Empire? ▼
Based on 2025 customer satisfaction surveys: Starlink ranks #1, T-Mobile Home Internet ranks #3. AT&T Fiber and Frontier both score above average. Spectrum and the legacy satellite providers (HughesNet, Viasat) rank lowest due to long hold times and limited technician availability. For outages in the last 30 days, see our Outages tab.
What happens after the promotional price ends? ▼
Spectrum's promo prices last 12 months, then increase by $20–35/mo. Frontier and AT&T Fiber prices never increase — they're flat for life. T-Mobile guarantees no increases for 5 years. Satellite providers (HughesNet, Viasat) often have 2-year contracts with price escalation. We always show the "regular rate" in our plan tables so you know exactly what to expect.
Can I get a bill credit for an internet outage? ▼
Yes — most ISPs will credit your bill for extended outages, but they rarely do it automatically. You have to request it. Call customer service, reference the outage date and duration, and ask for a service credit. Spectrum typically credits $5–10/day for verified outages. AT&T and Frontier will credit a prorated daily amount. You can also call us at (951) 713-7247 and we'll help you escalate.
Is this website affiliated with any internet provider? ▼
No — we're an independent comparison site. We earn a referral commission when you sign up through our links, but you pay the exact same price you'd pay going directly to the provider. We include all providers including ones where we earn nothing (like HughesNet and Viasat) because our goal is to give you accurate information. We're based in Riverside County and built this specifically for our community.

How to Cancel Every Internet Provider

Step-by-step cancellation guides for every ISP in Moreno Valley, Perris & Riverside. No surprises, no hidden fees — just exactly what to say and do. Data sourced from official provider policies and consumer advocacy guides (updated March 2026).

📞 Always get a confirmation number 📦 Return equipment within 30 days 🗓️ Time cancellation end of billing cycle

Spectrum
Cable • No contracts • No early termination fee
Medium
1
Prepare your account info
Have your account number (on your bill), the phone number on your account, and the last 4 digits of your SSN ready.
2
Call the cancellation line
Call (833) 267-6094. Online cancellation is not available. When asked why you're calling, say "service changes" — then select "remove service."
📞 (833) 267-6094
3
Beat the retention script
You'll be transferred to retention. They'll offer upgrades, discounts, and a pause option. If you're certain, say: "I'm moving out of Spectrum's service area" — this ends the script immediately.
💡 Saying "moving" = fastest path to cancellation
4
Get your confirmation
Ask for a cancellation confirmation number and email confirmation. Note your service end date — bills are not prorated, so time this to end of your billing cycle.
5
Return equipment
Return modem/router within 30 days at any Spectrum store or UPS/FedEx store (free). Keep your receipt — it's your proof against future equipment charges.
⚠️ Keep receipt — Spectrum has charged customers after return
6
Check your final bill
Log in or call back one billing cycle after cancellation to verify no new charges appear. Spectrum sometimes adds fees post-cancellation.
Early termination fee: None
Disconnect fee: None
Bill prorated? No — full month charged
Online cancel? No — call or store only

Frontier Fiber
Fiber • $9.99 disconnect fee • No contracts for most 2026 customers
Hard
1
Check if you're in a contract
Most customers who signed up after 2020 are contract-free. If you have a 1–2 year contract from 2019 or earlier, early termination fees of $200–$400 apply. Check your original agreement.
2
Pay off any disputes first
Frontier is more willing to negotiate outstanding balance disputes before you announce you're canceling. Resolve any billing disputes now for better results.
💡 Negotiate disputed charges before calling to cancel
3
Call to cancel — phone only
Call (800) 921-8101 and ask for the Retention Department. Alternatively, use the live chat at frontier.com/resources/cancel-service. Say you're moving to end retention scripts faster.
📞 (800) 921-8101
4
Set your cancellation date & get confirmation
Bills are not prorated — you're billed for the full final month regardless. Ask for a confirmation number and email receipt. Get the agent's name and ticket number.
5
Return equipment by UPS only
Frontier does not accept store drop-offs. Request a prepaid UPS return box — it arrives in 5–7 days. Return within 30 days or face $100–200/device fees. Keep the UPS tracking receipt.
⚠️ No store drop-offs — UPS mail only
6
Expect up to 3 more bills
After cancellation, you may receive up to 3 additional bills for residual charges. The $9.99 disconnect fee appears on your final bill. If on AutoPay, it will be auto-drafted — check your bank.
Disconnect fee: $9.99
ETF (if in contract): $200–$400
Bill prorated? No
Online cancel? No — phone or chat

AT&T Internet / Fiber
Fiber + 5G Air • No contracts • Equipment return within 21 days
Hard
1
Call during business hours — phone only
AT&T internet cancellations cannot be done online. Call (800) 288-2020 Mon–Fri 8am–7pm or Sat 8am–5pm local time. Say "cancel service" to the automated system to route directly to retention.
📞 (800) 288-2020
2
Have your credentials ready
You'll need your account number and the last 4 digits of your Social Security number. Budget 30–45 minutes — AT&T uses a multi-step retention script.
3
Decline the retention offers firmly
Expect 3+ counter-offers: a discount, a plan downgrade, and a transfer to a "specialist." Say firmly: "I appreciate it, but I've made my decision to cancel." Repeat as needed.
💡 "I've made my decision" — repeat calmly until they process it
4
Get your cancellation confirmation
Ask for a confirmation number and email receipt. Note your exact service end date. AT&T may prorate your final bill — you may receive a partial refund for unused days.
5
Return equipment within 21 days
Return the WiFi gateway and any extenders within 21 days (shorter than most providers). You can return at any AT&T store, UPS store, or FedEx location. Keep your tracking/drop-off receipt.
⚠️ 21-day return window — shorter than other ISPs
6
Monitor final 2 billing cycles
Watch for phantom charges — equipment fees, "restocking fees," or prorated amounts that shouldn't appear. If you see unauthorized charges, file at att.com/support or with the FCC at consumercomplaints.fcc.gov.
ETF: None (no contracts)
Disconnect fee: None
Bill prorated? Yes — may get refund
Equipment window: 21 days

Universal Cancellation Tips
Apply these to every ISP — they've saved customers hundreds of dollars.
🗓️
Time it to your billing cycle
Most ISPs (Spectrum, Frontier) don't prorate final bills. Cancel right before the end of your billing cycle to get full value from your last paid month.
📸
Document everything
Screenshot your account status before canceling. Get a confirmation number via email. Photograph equipment condition before boxing it up for return.
📦
Keep equipment return receipts forever
ISPs have been known to charge for equipment months after it was returned. Your UPS/FedEx receipt is the only proof you have — store it digitally.
💬
The magic phrase
"I'm moving out of the service area" ends retention scripts at Spectrum, Frontier, and AT&T faster than any other reason. Say it even if you're just switching providers.
💰
Request a bill credit for outages
Before canceling, ask for a bill credit for any recent outages. Most ISPs offer $5–10/day for verified outages. Call us at (951) 713-7247 and we'll help.
⚖️
Escalate disputes to the FCC
If an ISP charges fees you believe are wrong, file at consumercomplaints.fcc.gov. ISPs resolve FCC complaints quickly — it's their most effective accountability mechanism.
